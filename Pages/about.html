About Zawaad Khan

Born and raised in Bangladesh, I grew up in a society shaped by strong political and social controls. That early exposure to authoritarianism sharpened my belief in liberty, open societies, and the power of capitalism to drive real progress. Thinkers like Milton Friedman showed me how free markets and democratic institutions can hold power accountable and unlock prosperity.

I graduated from high school with High Honours and am now pursuing a degree in Economics at the University of Warwick. My near-term path includes working at Jane Street, earning an MBA from Harvard Business School, and building companies that set new global standards of excellence.

But my ambitions extend beyond finance. I aim to become one of the world’s most influential economists, a billionaire founder, and a public thinker who shapes both policy and geopolitics. Through patriotic and global philanthropy, I seek to advance economic opportunity, strong institutions, and long-term freedom.

My writing focuses on economics and geopolitics, drawing on the ideas of Milton Friedman, Peter Thiel, Thomas Sowell, and Stephen Kotkin. I write to clarify, challenge, and influence — because the future is up for grabs, and I intend to help shape it.

Born in Bangladesh in 2006, Zawaad Khan is an economics writer, entrepreneur, and strategist. His work spans finance, development, and geopolitics. This is the official site for updates, essays, and projects.
